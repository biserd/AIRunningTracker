You will organize the page into two distinct "chapters." Use a clear section header (H2) to visually separate them so the user knows they are switching context from "Speed" to "Safety."

---

#### Section 1: The Engine (Race Potential)
**Goal:** Answer *"How fit am I, and how fast can I race?"*
**Layout:**
* **Top Row (The "What"):**
    * **Race Predictions Card** (from `AI Insights`). This is the headline stat. Place it top-left or full width.
* **Middle Row (The "Why"):**
    * **VO2 Max Analysis** (from `Performance`). Place this directly next to or below Race Predictions.
    * *Why connect them?* If a user sees their prediction dropped, they immediately look at VO2 Max to understand if their fitness dropped.
* **Bottom Row (The "How"):**
    * **Heart Rate Zones** (from `Performance`). This explains the *training intensity* that contributes to the engine.

---

#### Section 2: The Mechanics (Form & Health)
**Goal:** Answer *"Am I running efficiently, or am I about to get injured?"*
**Layout:**
* **Top Row (The Risk):**
    * **Injury Risk Analysis** (from `AI Insights`). This is the most critical alert. It acts as the "Check Engine Light."
* **Bottom Row (The Cause):**
    * **Running Efficiency** (Cadence, Oscillation, etc. from `Performance`).
    * *Why connect them?* The "Risk Factors" in your AI card often cite things like "inconsistent volume" or biomechanics. Placing the **Cadence/Oscillation** metrics right below the Injury Risk helps the user see *what* to fix to lower their risk.
